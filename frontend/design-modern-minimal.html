About Georgia

8,000 years of wine,
culture and warm hearts.

Georgia is often called the cradle of wine — its ancient qvevri clay-pot winemaking tradition is a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. But wine is just the beginning. Here, medieval fortresses crown every ridge, polyphonic choirs echo through stone churches, and the table, the supra, is a sacred ritual of friendship.

From the snow-capped peaks of Kazbegi and the alpine meadows of Mestia, to the Black Sea warmth of Batumi and the vine-striped valleys of Kakheti — Georgia compresses an entire continent of landscapes into a country smaller than Ireland.
