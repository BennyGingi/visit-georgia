The Greater Caucasus

Towering peaks pierce the clouds at over 5,000 meters. Ancient watchtowers guard forgotten valleys. The mountains of Georgia have watched empires rise and fall in silence.

The Cradle of Wine

Eight thousand years before Bordeaux, Georgians were fermenting grapes in clay qvevri buried in the earth. 525 indigenous grape varieties. One unbroken tradition of hospitality.

A Living Culture

Polyphonic singing listed by UNESCO. Script older than most alphabets. Orthodox churches built into cliffsides. Georgia carries its identity not in museums, but in daily life.
